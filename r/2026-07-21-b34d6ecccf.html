Top 3
1

Cursor: agent swarms and the new model economics

Cursor published research showing a planner/worker split — expensive frontier models decompose intent, cheap fast models execute — hitting similar quality at ~8x lower cost ($1,339 vs $10,565 on the same SQLite task). Worker tokens were 69–90% of volume but planner tokens drove cost.
⚡ For you: Rethink how you'll architect HIH's agentic features and their unit economics — don't route every step to the top model; design a planner/worker topology and treat crisp intent specs as the leverage point. This is the cost model that decides whether AI-native diagnostics/pharmacy agents are viable at India scale.
